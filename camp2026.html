It was a rainy few days leading up to our move-in this year. I still had PTSD from 2019 and was nervous about how the grounds would be when we got there. Thankfully, it was nothing we couldn't handle with a little 4 wheel drive, momentum, and the kind of driving skill that makes women flock to our site. Others had a tougher time in their motorhomes. But most people got to their spots just fine and the track is always good about having an operator on-site to pull folks out with the skidsteer.

We got settled in our usual spot in the North West corner of 1A. We arrived even earlier than usual this year and still only barely got where we wanted. May have to get up even earlier in '27! Brought two campers again this year. It's nice that we have grown and don't have to share beds anymore. Mowed our site and took part in everyone's favorite Thursday tradition...sitting around and waiting. There's not much to do on Thursday.

The Hoosier Hundred at IRP was postponed to June and the Carb Night Classic Freedom 75 & Freedom 90 were moved up a day. So I went out to Indianapolis Raceway Park a day early.

My expectations for a crowd were low, but it was pitiful. Which is a shame, because those USF2000 guys put on a couple of great races! The concession food was terrible as usual. Someday I will learn my lesson and bring my own food.
