39th International 500-Mile Sweepstakes

Background

For Bob Sweikert, May of 1954 started in tears. Those tears were for the misfortune of a good friend, Bob Scott. Late on the final day of qualifying for the 38th International 500-Mile Sweepstakes, it became clear that Scott’s qualifying time was not going to get him into the race. He was bumped from the field and Sweikert hustled and got his friend a ride in the no. 18 Ray Brady owned car. With only minutes remaining, Scott made it on track just in time. His first three laps (138.419, 139.018, and 136.778) indicated he would be fast enough to make the field. But then, after taking the white flag, he backed off the throttle. Slowly, he came back to the pits, one lap short. He reported he had become confused by the white signal flag in the late-evening shadows, believing it to be the checkered flag. Additionally, the happiness of many pulling for him at the starting line had confused him. Sweikert, thrilled that his friend would make the race, was giving a congratulatory fighter’s handshake – meaning not to indicate that the qualifying was over, but that he was doing fine. Scott came in and cried for a long time and apologized to many people he believed he had let down. He made it known he didn’t want anyone to blame Sweikert but rather himself. “Scott’s such a great guy,” Sweikert said. “He passed up sprints this month just so he could spend all his time here at the track. His wife’s expecting and money is scarce. Maybe there’ll be relief jobs for the race but what good is that?” Bob Scott would be killed in a crash at Darlington Raceway over a month later. He was 25 years old.
